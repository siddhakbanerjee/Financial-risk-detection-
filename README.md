# Financial-risk-detection-
This reserach   is identifying  the risk before  granting  any loan application. 
**Problem statement **
Modern lending requires more than just a credit score; it requires a multidimensional understanding of risk. The primary goal of this project was to build a robust analytical framework to identify patterns in loan defaults. By correlating variables such as employment type, age group, YOY financial shifts, and credit bins, the project provides a granular view of which segments are most susceptible to financial instability. This helps in refining credit scoring models and optimizing loan approval workflows
Key Insights & Visualizations:

Loan Default and Overview: Provides a high-level view of loan purposes and employment types. Notably, it highlights that while full-time employees have the highest average income, unemployed applicants exhibit the highest default rates at 3.4%.

Applicant Demographics & Financial Profile: Deep dives into credit score distributions and educational backgrounds. The data shows a significant concentration of total loan volume in the "Medium" and "High" credit score bins, totaling over 8.6bn.

Financial Risk Metrics: Analyzes Year-on-Year (YOY) changes and Year-to-Date (YTD) performance. This section tracks the volatility of default loans, such as the 2.7% increase seen in 2015, and maps the flow of loan amounts across credit categories and marital statuses.
